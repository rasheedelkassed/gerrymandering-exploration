It's Not Arbitrary

An explanation of the unexplanable electoral boundaries

You've just been tasked with drawing the electoral boundaries for an entire state of the GSA, better known as The Grided States of America.

In an attempt to provide proper representation of the general population, the GSA divides its states with the usage of electoral districts. These districts are then given a representative with a political affiliation that matches the winning majority of the district. The number and size of the districts that a state gets are determined by their population.

Each state consists of a several square neighborhoods which follow one of the two main political parties: Purple or Yellow, and it's your job to draw the electoral boundaries such that there is a proper amount of representation.
